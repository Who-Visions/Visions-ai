## SECTION II: CRITICAL ENGAGEMENT (25%)

**Question 3 (12%):**
**Falsification Challenge:**

Arnheim claims "simplicity is the dominant principle of perception" (both works). However:
- Baroque art deliberately creates visual complexity
- Some cultures prefer maximalist aesthetics (Indian, Islamic)  
- Natural scenes are statistically complex yet easily perceived

**Your task:**
a) Identify the THREE strongest counter-examples to Arnheim's simplicity principle
b) Defend Arnheim against these critiques OR propose a refinement of his theory
c) Design an experiment that would FALSIFY the simplicity principle if your prediction is wrong

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Intellectual honesty (can you steel-man the opposition?)
- Theoretical sophistication (going beyond Arnheim)
- Methodological rigor (actual experiment design, not speculation)

**Question 4 (13%):**
**The Ontological Status of Perceptual Forces**

Arnheim uses "force," "tension," "weight," "pull" throughout both works. Are these:
a) Metaphors (convenient but scientifically meaningless)?
b) Phenomenological descriptions (subjectively real, objectively absent)?
c) Physical reductions (literally neural firing patterns)?
d) Sui generis perceptual properties (a distinct ontological category)?

**Defend your position** with:
- Analysis of Arnheim's actual usage across 10+ passages
- Engagement with philosophy of mind (Searle, Dennett, Chalmers)

- Original theoretical contribution

## ORAL DEFENSE SECTION (20 minutes each committee member)

After written exam, candidates present their dissertation proposal to the examining committee. Each member asks follow-up questions testing:

1. **Deep Understanding**: Can you explain Arnheim better than Arnheim?
2. **Critical Thinking**: What are the weaknesses in your own proposal?
3. **Interdisciplinary Breadth**: How does your work connect to neuroscience, computer science, philosophy, cultural studies?
4. **Research Methodology**: Why should we believe your results will be meaningful?
5. **Contribution to Knowledge**: What do we learn that we didn't know before?

**Committee members will:**
- Identify contradictions in your written answers
- Pose hypothetical counter-examples
- Ask you to defend positions you didn't explicitly take
- Demand on-the-spot theoretical innovation

**Passing requires:**
- Flawless responses to 90%+ of questions
- Demonstrating intellectual humility when wrong
- Showing capacity for original thought under pressure
