### **Workflow: The Pre-Session Consultation Call**

**Goal:** To "keep the lid on the can of worms" (avoid chaos) by setting strict expectations regarding hair, makeup, and clothing *before* the client arrives at the studio.

#### **1. The Male Client Protocol ("Burt")**
*   **0:51 - Makeup Strategy:**
    *   **Rule:** Advise against professional makeup for men.
    *   **Reasoning:** It often looks detectable. Blemishes should be handled in post-production (Photoshop).
    *   **Exceptions:** Only use makeup if the client has a severe skin condition or massive blemishes.
    *   **On-Set Tools:** Have **moisturizer** (for dry skin), **toner/powder** (for oily skin), and **lip balm** ready.
*   **1:18 - Hair Preparation:**
    *   **Instruction:** Ask the client to arrive with *less* product than usual.
    *   **Workflow:** Bring their hair product with them. Start dry/natural and add product incrementally during the shoot.
    *   **Avoid:** The "wet/nappy" look caused by arriving with too much product.
*   **2:34 - Facial Hair:**
    *   **Instruction:** If they can grow scruff/beard, arrive with it.
    *   **Reasoning:** Facial hair absorbs light and adds shape to the face.
    *   **Workflow:** Shoot the scruffy look first, then have the client shave mid-session for a clean look.
    *   **Tool:** Client must bring their own grooming kit/razor.
*   **3:01 - Wardrobe (Layering is Key):**
    *   **Quantity:** Request 10â€“15 options for a full session (5â€“10 for a 1-hour session).
    *   **Variety:** Range from casual (T-shirts) to upscale (Suit/Tie).
    *   **The Layering System:**
        1.  T-shirts (Crew and V-neck).
        2.  Collared shirts / Button-downs / Polos.
        3.  Sweaters.
        4.  Light Jackets (Avoid puffy/bulky jackets).
    *   **Colors:** Neutrals are preferred, but bright colors are acceptable. Avoid busy patterns unless the client loves them.

#### **2. The Female Client Protocol ("Phyllis")**
*   **5:18 - Makeup Arrival:**
    *   **Rule:** Arrive with a completely clean face. No residue, no leftover mascara.
    *   **Tool:** Client should bring **makeup remover** if coming from work/auditions.
*   **5:56 - Makeup Progression:**
    *   **Workflow:** Start with a "Light/Natural" base.
    *   **Progression:** Add makeup as the session moves forward.
    *   **End State:** Finish with the darkest/smokiest eye or lip for a "sexy/sophisticated" look.
*   **6:48 - Wardrobe:**
    *   **Instruction:** "No rules." Bring a massive range of options.
    *   **Logistics:** Don't worry about wrinkles; the studio has a **steamer**.
*   **7:12 - Hair Preparation:**
    *   **Rule:** Arrive having a "Good Hair Day."
    *   **Instruction:** If the client needs a blowout, they must get it *before* arriving.
    *   **Reasoning:** The studio makeup artist is there for tweaking and fine-tuning, not for fixing "bedhead" or starting from scratch.
    *   **On-Set Workflow:** Start with hair down, move to ponytail/updo later (ponytails are great for headshots).

#### **3. The "Energy Reset" Technique**
*   **9:40 - Managing Session Energy:**
    *   **Trigger:** If the photographer feels the energy draining or the client becoming stiff.
    *   **Action:** Tell the client to "Go Change."
    *   **Result:** Changing clothes acts as a mental reset, bringing the client back with fresh energy.

#### **4. The "Ownership" Philosophy**
*   **11:28 - Liability Management:**
    *   **Concept:** By discussing these details beforehand, you shift the responsibility of the "base look" to the client.
    *   **Outcome:** If the client arrives looking disheveled or unprepared, it is on them, not the photographer. This prevents post-shoot complaints like "That doesn't look like my hair."
