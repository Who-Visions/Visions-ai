### **Tools Required**
*   **Camera:** DSLR or Mirrorless (Canon used in video).
*   **Lighting:** Studio lighting (Kino Flo continuous lights used in video, but applies to strobes/softboxes).
*   **Subject:** Person wearing glasses (various anti-reflective coatings).

### **Core Concept: The Three Variables**
To eliminate glare without compromising the lighting style, you have control over three specific variables. You should adjust them in this order:
1.  **The Lights** (Position/Angle)
2.  **The Subject** (Head Angle)
3.  **The Glasses** (Physical Angle on Face)

---

### **Workflow: Eliminating Glasses Glare**

#### **1. Initial Diagnosis (00:54 - 01:19)**
*   Take a test shot with your standard lighting setup.
*   **Analyze:** Zoom in on the eyes. Identify exactly where the softbox/light source is reflecting (e.g., directly over the pupil, on the frame rim, or covering the iris).
*   *Note:* Different lens coatings (green, purple, clear) behave differently.

#### **2. Method A: Adjust Light Height (02:28 - 03:22)**
*   **Action:** Raise your main light source higher.
*   **Technique:** While looking at the subject, physically lift the light stand.
*   **Visual Cue:** Watch the reflection in the glasses. You want the bottom edge of the light reflection to sit **just at the top of the frame** or the very top of the eye, clearing the pupil.
*   **Trade-off:** Raising the light too high may increase shadows under the nose or chin. You may need to angle the light down slightly after raising it.

#### **3. Method B: Adjust Subject Head Angle (03:23 - 04:01)**
*   **Action:** Instruct the subject to lower their chin.
*   **Command:** "Chin down slightly."
*   **Why it works:** This changes the angle of incidence, reflecting the light downward instead of back into the camera lens.
*   **Refinement:** Have them tilt their head until the glare moves out of the critical eye area.

#### **4. Method C: The "Glasses Tilt" Trick (04:29 - 06:27)**
*   *Use this if moving the light or chin affects the look of the portrait too negatively.*
*   **Action:** Change the trajectory of the glass lenses by tilting them forward.
*   **Technique:** Ask the subject to lift the temple arms (earpieces) of the glasses **off their ears**.
    *   Rest the arms about an inch above the ears.
    *   This angles the lenses downward toward the floor.
*   **Result:** The camera angle (straight on) cannot see that the glasses are floating off the ears, but the downward angle of the glass kills the reflection entirely.
*   **Verification:** Take a shot and zoom in. The glare should be gone, and the glasses should still look natural.

#### **5. The "Combo" Adjustment (06:47 - 08:18)**
*   *Scenario:* If the subject changes glasses or has difficult lenses, one method may not suffice.
*   **Step 1:** Apply **Method C** (Lift glasses off ears).
*   **Step 2:** Apply **Method B** (Chin down slightly).
*   **Step 3:** Apply **Method A** (Raise lights slightly if a sliver of glare remains).
*   **Goal:** Find the balance where the eyes are clear, but the lighting on the face remains flattering (avoiding deep shadows in eye sockets).

### **Summary Checklist (08:51 - 09:32)**
1.  **Move the Light:** Raise it up.
2.  **Move the Person:** Chin down / forehead forward.
3.  **Move the Glasses:** Lift arms off ears to tilt lenses down.
