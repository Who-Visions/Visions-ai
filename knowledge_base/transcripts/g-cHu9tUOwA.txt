### **Workflow: The Peter Hurley Headshot Method**

#### **1. Subject Psychology & Diffusion**
*   **Timestamp:** 00:00 - 00:12
*   **Concept:** The primary role of the headshot photographer is not technical, but psychological.
*   **Action:** Identify that the subject arrives "uncomfortable, stiff, scared, uncertain, and petrified."
*   **Step:** The photographer must actively "diffuse the situation" before expecting a quality image. This involves conversation and energy management before the camera is even raised.

#### **2. The Lighting Setup (Continuous LED)**
*   **Timestamp:** 00:27, 00:37, 00:44
*   **Tools:** Westcott Flex Cine Mats (LED).
*   **Configuration:**
    *   **Key/Fill:** The video shows a multi-light setup surrounding the subject's face to eliminate shadows and create a clean, high-key look.
    *   **Positioning:** Lights are placed in a square or triangular formation (often referred to as the "Hurley Shabang" setup in his broader teachings).
    *   **Distance:** The lights are placed very close to the subject (within arm's reach) to create soft light and large catchlights in the eyes.

#### **3. Micro-Coaching & Direction**
*   **Timestamp:** 00:17 - 00:19
*   **Action:** Use specific, micro-adjustments rather than broad posing commands.
*   **Commands Heard:**
    *   "Chin down."
    *   "A little bit up with the [chin/head]."
    *   "Nose a little bit that way."
*   **Goal:** These small movements are designed to accentuate the jawline and engage the eyes (the "Squinch" technique, implied by the visual results).

#### **4. Tethered Shooting & Review**
*   **Timestamp:** 00:36, 1:13
*   **Tools:** Tether Tools Cable (High-visibility Orange), Laptop/Monitor.
*   **Workflow:**
    *   The camera is physically connected to a computer.
    *   Images are reviewed instantly on a large screen.
    *   **Purpose:** This allows the subject to see what the photographer sees, helping to coach them into better expressions by showing them the difference between a "scared" look and a confident one.
