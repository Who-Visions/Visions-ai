### **Workflow: The BHAG & PTAG Achievement System**

This workflow outlines the psychological framework and practical steps for achieving high-level success, moving from visualization to granular execution.

#### **1. Establish the Visualization Foundation**
Before setting specific goals, establish a mental baseline of success using sports psychology techniques.
*   **Technique:** Visualization and "Law of Attraction."
*   **Action:** Visualize the end result and the performance required to get there before the event occurs.
*   **Goal:** Build "Belief Expectancy"â€”the internal knowing that the outcome is already achieved.
*   **Timestamp:** 00:40 - 01:15

#### **2. Define Your BHAG (Big Hairy Audacious Goal)**
Set the primary objective. This goal should be massive and intimidating.
*   **Tool:** **BHAG** (Big Hairy Audacious Goal).
*   **Parameters:**
    *   Must be outside your current "Comfort Zone."
    *   Must be "scary enough" to provoke fear (if it isn't scary, it isn't big enough).
    *   *Example:* Making the Olympic Team, becoming the #1 professional in your city, changing careers entirely.
*   **Timestamp:** 05:50 - 06:15 / 10:45

#### **3. The "Turning Point" Decision**
A BHAG remains a dream until a specific decision is made. This step distinguishes "dreaming" (wallowing) from "goals" (action).
*   **Action:** Make a binary decision to commit to the BHAG, even if the path isn't fully clear yet.
*   **Key Concept:** Acknowledge that 90% of worries never happen; ignore the fear of the "how" and commit to the "what."
*   **Timestamp:** 03:27 - 04:38 / 09:30

#### **4. Break Down into PTAGs (Progress Tracking Attainable Goals)**
Deconstruct the massive BHAG into small, immediate, and logistical steps.
*   **Tool:** **PTAG** (Progress Tracking Attainable Goal).
*   **Action:** List the immediate physical actions required to move toward the BHAG.
*   **Examples (Peter's Sailing PTAGs):**
    *   Ship equipment to the training location.
    *   Secure housing in the new city.
    *   Register for the World Championship.
*   **Examples (Anita's Photography PTAGs):**
    *   Shoot anyone available (friends, kids) to build a portfolio.
    *   Master lighting techniques.
    *   Learn to take criticism/feedback.
*   **Timestamp:** 06:40 - 07:00 / 08:50

#### **5. Execute "The Grind" (Volume & Iteration)**
Execute the PTAGs with high volume to build competence.
*   **Action:** Do the work regardless of fear or lack of perfect equipment.
*   **Anita's Workflow:**
    *   Shoot on "Auto" if necessary to start.
    *   Transition to manual/lighting as confidence builds.
    *   Shoot high volume (anyone and everyone) until the skill set matches the ambition.
*   **Timestamp:** 08:50 - 09:25

#### **6. The Reset (Avoiding Stagnation)**
Once a BHAG is achieved, you must immediately reset to avoid spinning in a new comfort zone.
*   **Trigger:** Achievement of the primary goal.
*   **Action:** Create a *new* BHAG immediately.
*   **Warning:** If you do not have a new goal after succeeding, you will stagnate.
*   **Timestamp:** 11:15 - 11:30 / 14:10
