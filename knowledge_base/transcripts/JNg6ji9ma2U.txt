*   **Lighting:** Studio strobe/flash (e.g., Profoto B1/B10 as seen in video).

### **Workflow: The "Camera Talk" Technique**

#### **1. The Pre-Flight Check (The Hurley System)**
Before engaging in "Camera Talk," ensure the foundation of the shot is established.
*   **00:37** **Position the Body:** Set the subject's stance and angle relative to the camera.
*   **00:39** **Position the Head:** Direct the chin, tilt, and rotation.
*   **00:41** **Scan Hair & Clothing:** visually sweep the subject to ensure no stray hairs or wardrobe malfunctions.
*   **00:43** **Compose the Shot:** Frame the image in the viewfinder.

#### **2. Initial Coaching (Off-Camera Interaction)**
Use this phase for teaching concepts or physical mirroring.
*   **01:13** **Establish Eye Contact:** Step away from the camera initially to build rapport.
*   **01:52** **Mirroring:** If you need the client to mimic a physical move (e.g., "Jawline out"), step away from the camera so they can see your body language and mirror it.
*   **03:10** **Educational Coaching:** If teaching a specific technique they will use throughout the shoot (e.g., The Squinch, The Minuscule Smile), do this face-to-face, away from the viewfinder.

#### **3. Engaging "Camera Talk" (The Shooting Phase)**
Once the shot is composed, the photographer must transition to "Camera Talk" to maintain the energy loop.
*   **01:35** **Lock In:** Place your eye to the viewfinder and stay there. Do not lower the camera between every shot.
*   **02:15** **The Stimulus Loop:**
    1.  **Verbal Command:** Give a direction (e.g., "Turn left," "Drop shoulder," "Suck in gut") while looking through the lens.
    2.  **Reaction:** Wait for the subject's micro-expression change.
    3.  **Fire:** Take the photo immediately upon reaction.
    4.  **Repeat:** Immediately give the next command without moving your head from the camera.
*   **02:40** **Volume Adjustment:** Speak louder than usual. The camera body acts as a physical barrier between your mouth and the subject; project your voice so they are forced to "look through the lens" to listen to you.

#### **4. Maintaining Rhythm**
*   **03:45** **The Hot Seat Rule:** If the client is in the lights, they are in the "Hot Seat." Do not break the flow by stepping back unless absolutely necessary.
*   **04:00** **Rapid Fire Direction:**
    *   *Incorrect:* Give direction -> Lower camera -> Talk -> Raise camera -> Shoot.
    *   *Correct:* Direction -> Shoot -> Direction -> Shoot.
*   **04:26** **Timing:** Use "Camera Talk" to establish a rhythm where the subject is constantly reacting to your voice coming from behind the lens.

### **Key Parameters & Cues**

*   **The Squinch (03:17):** Narrowing the lower eyelids to convey confidence (teach this off-camera first).
*   **The Minuscule Smile (03:24):** A tiny smile with the mouth closed to soften the expression (teach this off-camera first).
*   **Proximity (01:00):** During the shooting phase, the camera should be glued to your face, "locked and loaded."
*   **Audio Projection:** Increase vocal volume by roughly **20-30%** to overcome the camera blocking your face and to command attention through the lens.
